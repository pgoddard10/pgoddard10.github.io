Seeing Art Differently: How can visual art be enhanced for people with low vision?

They say beauty is in the eye of the beholderâ€”but what if that eye has low vision? Many accessibility tools for people with low vision rely on non-visual senses, like audio descriptions, but this research takes a different approach. This project explores ways to enhance artwork visually using Augmented Reality (AR). With an AR headset, people with low vision view art through translucent lenses while a camera captures the scene, processes it, and re-displays enhancements in real time. Adjustments like increased contrast or edge enhancements (shown here) reveal previously hidden details. Through participatory design, we collaborate with people with low vision to understand the challenges they face when viewing art and refine solutions that make it more inclusive.
